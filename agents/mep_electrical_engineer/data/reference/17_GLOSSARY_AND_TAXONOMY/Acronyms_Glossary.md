## Common MEP Terms

### Electrical

- **Ampacity** - Current-carrying capacity of a conductor in amperes
- **Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ)** - Organization/official with legal authority to enforce codes
- **Branch Circuit** - Circuit conductors between the final overcurrent device and the outlets
- **Coordination Study** - Analysis ensuring upstream protective devices operate after downstream devices
- **Demand Factor** - Ratio of maximum demand to total connected load
- **Diversity Factor** - Ratio of sum of individual maximum demands to maximum demand of the whole system
- **Feeder** - Circuit conductors between service equipment and branch circuit overcurrent device
- **Ground Fault** - Unintentional path between phase conductor and ground
- **Load Calculation** - Process of determining electrical load for sizing service, feeders, and branch circuits
- **One-Line Diagram** - Simplified electrical distribution system diagram
- **Service** - Conductors and equipment delivering energy from utility to building wiring
- **Short Circuit** - Abnormal low-resistance connection between conductors of different potential
- **Voltage Drop** - Reduction in voltage along a conductor due to impedance and current flow

### Mechanical (HVAC)

- **Chiller** - Refrigeration machine that removes heat from liquid (usually water)
- **Coefficient of Performance (COP)** - Ratio of useful heating/cooling to energy input
- **Condenser** - Component that rejects heat from refrigeration cycle
- **Evaporator** - Component that absorbs heat in refrigeration cycle
- **Heat Pump** - Refrigeration system that can reverse to provide heating or cooling
- **HVAC Load** - Amount of heating/cooling required to maintain comfort conditions
- **Psychrometric Chart** - Graph showing relationships between air properties
- **Tonnage** - Cooling capacity; 1 ton = 12,000 BTU/hr
- **Wet Bulb Temperature** - Lowest temperature achievable by evaporative cooling

### Plumbing

- **Backflow** - Flow of water or other substances into potable water supply from undesirable sources
- **Drainage Fixture Unit (DFU)** - Measure of probable discharge into drainage system
- **Fixture** - Device that requires connection to water supply and discharge to drain
- **P-Trap** - U-shaped pipe that creates water seal to prevent sewer gas entry
- **Potable Water** - Water suitable for human consumption
- **Sanitary Sewer** - System carrying human waste and wastewater
- **Storm Drain** - System carrying rainwater and surface water
- **Vent** - Pipe providing circulation of air to/from drainage system
- **Water Supply Fixture Unit (WSFU)** - Measure of probable water demand

### Fire Protection

- **Design Area** - Portion of sprinkler system expected to operate in a fire
- **Density** - Rate of water application over a specified area (gpm/sq ft)
- **Hydraulic Calculation** - Method of sizing pipe to deliver required flow and pressure
- **Sprinkler Head** - Device that discharges water when activated by heat
- **Standpipe** - System of piping, valves, and hose connections for fire department use
- **Wet System** - Sprinkler system with water under pressure at all times
- **Dry System** - Sprinkler system with air/nitrogen under pressure; water held back by valve

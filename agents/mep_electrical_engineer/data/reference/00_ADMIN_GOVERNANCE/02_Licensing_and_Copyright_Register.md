## Purpose and Legal Framework

### Purpose

This document establishes mandatory policies and procedures for copyright compliance, licensing, and attribution within the MEP Engineering Knowledge Base. It ensures:

- **Legal Compliance**: Adherence to copyright law and intellectual property rights
- **Risk Mitigation**: Protection against copyright infringement claims
- **Professional Ethics**: Respect for intellectual property of standards organizations and authors
- **Transparency**: Clear licensing terms for original and derived content
- **Attribution**: Proper credit to sources and contributors

### Legal Framework

This policy is based on:

- **U.S. Copyright Act (17 U.S.C.)**: Federal copyright law
- **Fair Use Doctrine (17 U.S.C. ¬ß 107)**: Limitations on exclusive rights
- **Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA)**: Digital copyright provisions
- **Creative Commons Licenses**: Standard public copyright licenses
- **Professional Engineering Codes of Ethics**: NSPE, IEEE, and state licensing boards

### Definitions

**Copyright**: Legal right granting creator exclusive control over use and distribution of original work

**Fair Use**: Legal doctrine allowing limited use of copyrighted material without permission for purposes such as commentary, criticism, education, and research

**Public Domain**: Works not protected by copyright, freely usable by anyone

**Open Source**: Software/content with source code/text available under licenses permitting modification and redistribution

**Attribution**: Acknowledgment of original source and creator of work

**Derivative Work**: Work based on or derived from existing copyrighted work

**Paraphrase**: Restatement of text in different words while preserving meaning

## Fair Use Doctrine Application

### Four-Factor Fair Use Analysis

Fair use determination requires analysis of four statutory factors:

#### Factor 1: Purpose and Character of Use

**Favors Fair Use**:
- Educational, instructional, or informational purpose
- Nonprofit use
- Transformative use (adds new meaning, insight, or understanding)
- Commentary, criticism, or analysis

**Disfavors Fair Use**:
- Commercial purpose
- Mere reproduction without transformation
- Substitute for original work

**Application in Knowledge Base**:
‚úÖ Educational purpose (training engineers)
‚úÖ Transformative (applying codes to specific scenarios, providing context)
‚úÖ Commentary (explaining how to apply requirements)

#### Factor 2: Nature of Copyrighted Work

**Favors Fair Use**:
- Factual works (vs. creative works)
- Published works (vs. unpublished)
- Works necessary for public interest (e.g., safety codes)

**Disfavors Fair Use**:
- Highly creative works
- Unpublished works
- Works where author expects licensing revenue

**Application in Knowledge Base**:
‚úÖ NEC and codes are factual/technical (not creative)
‚úÖ Published and publicly available (for purchase)
‚ö†Ô∏è Standards organizations rely on sales revenue (use cautiously)

#### Factor 3: Amount and Substantiality

**Favors Fair Use**:
- Small portion of work used
- Only amount necessary for purpose
- Not "heart" of the work

**Disfavors Fair Use**:
- Large portion or entire work reproduced
- Most valuable or distinctive part used
- Amount exceeds what's necessary

**Application in Knowledge Base**:
‚úÖ Brief excerpts only (1-2 sentences max)
‚úÖ Paraphrased summaries instead of verbatim text
‚úÖ Reference specific sections without reproducing full text
‚ùå Do not reproduce entire articles, tables, or chapters

**Guideline**: Use no more than 10% of any copyrighted work, and never more than necessary to convey the concept.

#### Factor 4: Effect on Market Value

**Favors Fair Use**:
- No substitute for original
- Does not reduce demand for original
- Might increase interest in original

**Disfavors Fair Use**:
- Acts as substitute (users don't need original)
- Reduces sales or licensing opportunities
- Harms potential market

**Application in Knowledge Base**:
‚úÖ Engineers still need official code books for comprehensive reference
‚úÖ Knowledge base complements (not replaces) codes
‚úÖ May increase awareness and proper use of codes
‚ùå Do not create comprehensive restatements that eliminate need for codes

### Fair Use Best Practices for Knowledge Base

**DO**:
- ‚úÖ Cite specific NEC/code sections by number (e.g., "NEC 210.19(A)(1)")
- ‚úÖ Paraphrase requirements in your own words
- ‚úÖ Provide context, examples, and application guidance
- ‚úÖ Use brief quotations (1-2 sentences) when necessary for clarity
- ‚úÖ Add transformative content (interpretations, calculations, comparisons)
- ‚úÖ Link to official sources for full text

**DON'T**:
- ‚ùå Copy entire code articles or sections verbatim
- ‚ùå Reproduce code tables without permission
- ‚ùå Create comprehensive code summaries that substitute for official text
- ‚ùå Scan and distribute copyrighted documents
- ‚ùå Circumvent paywalls or access controls

### Example: Fair Use Compliant Content

**‚ùå PROHIBITED (Verbatim Reproduction)**:
```
"210.19(A)(1) General. Branch-circuit conductors shall have an ampacity
not less than the maximum load to be served. Conductors of multioutlet
branch circuits supplying receptacles for cord-and-plug-connected portable
loads shall have an ampacity of not less than the rating of the branch circuit."
```

**‚úÖ PERMITTED (Paraphrased with Attribution)**:
```
NEC 210.19(A)(1) requires branch-circuit conductors to have ampacity at
least equal to the maximum load they serve. For multioutlet circuits with
portable loads, conductor ampacity must meet or exceed the branch circuit
rating. For example, a 20A circuit requires conductors rated for at least 20A.

Reference: NFPA 70¬Æ, National Electrical Code¬Æ, 2023 Edition, Section 210.19(A)(1).
```

**‚úÖ PERMITTED (Interpretive Commentary)**:
```
When sizing branch circuit conductors per NEC Article 210, engineers must
ensure the conductor ampacity meets the maximum expected load. The Code
(Section 210.19(A)(1)) establishes this as a minimum requirement to prevent
conductor overheating. In practice, this means:

1. Calculate the connected load (VA or amperes)
2. Apply demand factors if permitted (NEC 220)
3. Select conductor size with ampacity ‚â• calculated load
4. Verify compliance with OCPD coordination requirements

Reference: NEC 2023, Article 210.19
```

## Prohibited Content and Practices

### Strictly Prohibited

The following are **absolutely prohibited** in the knowledge base:

#### 1. Verbatim Code Reproduction

‚ùå **PROHIBITED**:
- Copying entire NEC articles, sections, or subsections word-for-word
- Reproducing code tables in their entirety
- Creating "unofficial code books" by aggregating code text
- Scanning and uploading copyrighted code documents

**Penalty**: Immediate removal; potential legal liability

#### 2. Plagiarism

‚ùå **PROHIBITED**:
- Presenting others' work as your own
- Copying text from sources without attribution
- Paraphrasing without citation
- Using others' ideas without credit

**Penalty**: Content rejection; possible disciplinary action

#### 3. Unauthorized Derivative Works

‚ùå **PROHIBITED**:
- Creating modified versions of copyrighted calculators or tools without permission
- Reverse engineering proprietary software and redistributing
- Translating copyrighted works without authorization

**Penalty**: Content removal; legal review

#### 4. Copyright Circumvention

‚ùå **PROHIBITED**:
- Sharing login credentials for paywalled resources
- Distributing copyrighted PDFs or scans
- Using VPNs or proxies to bypass access controls
- Encouraging piracy or unauthorized access

**Penalty**: Access revocation; possible termination

### Gray Areas Requiring Legal Review

The following require **legal review before inclusion**:

‚ö†Ô∏è **REQUIRES REVIEW**:
- Reproducing more than 10% of any copyrighted work
- Using copyrighted images, diagrams, or figures
- Creating comprehensive summaries that might substitute for originals
- Translations of copyrighted works
- Content with unclear copyright status

**Process**: Submit to Knowledge Manager for legal review (see Section 10)

## Compliance Procedures

### Pre-Publication Copyright Review

**All content must pass copyright review before publication.**

#### Self-Review Checklist (Creators)

Before submitting content, verify:

- [ ] All sources properly cited
- [ ] No verbatim code text exceeding 1-2 sentences
- [ ] Tables and data paraphrased or derived (not copied)
- [ ] Images/diagrams are original or properly licensed
- [ ] Fair use principles followed (see Section 3)
- [ ] Attribution templates used
- [ ] License terms specified for original content

#### QA Review (Reviewers)

QA reviewers verify:

- [ ] Citations complete and properly formatted
- [ ] No copyright violations detected
- [ ] Fair use analysis supports included excerpts
- [ ] Attribution adequate for all external sources
- [ ] Licensing terms clear and appropriate

### Post-Publication Monitoring

**Ongoing Compliance**:
- Quarterly audits of random sample (10% of documents)
- Annual comprehensive review of all content
- User reporting mechanism for suspected violations
- Periodic updates when code editions change

### Copyright Complaint Response

If copyright complaint received:

1. **Immediate Action** (within 24 hours):
   - Remove or restrict access to contested content
   - Notify Knowledge Manager and Legal Counsel
   - Document complaint details

2. **Investigation** (within 5 business days):
   - Review content against fair use criteria
   - Assess validity of complaint
   - Determine appropriate response

3. **Resolution** (within 15 business days):
   - **If complaint valid**: Remove content permanently, apologize, implement corrective measures
   - **If complaint invalid**: Restore access, respond to complainant with justification
   - **If uncertain**: Seek legal counsel, err on side of caution

# International Building Code (IBC) 2024 - MEP Coordination Index

## Document Overview

**Standard**: International Building Code (IBC) 2024 Edition
**Publisher**: International Code Council (ICC)
**Scope**: Comprehensive building safety regulations for new and existing structures
**MEP Relevance**: Foundational requirements for mechanical, electrical, plumbing, and fire protection systems integration

## Purpose

The IBC establishes minimum requirements for building systems and construction to safeguard public health, safety, and general welfare through structural strength, means of egress facilities, stability, sanitation, adequate light and ventilation, energy conservation, and safety to life and property from fire and other hazards.

## Chapter 3: Occupancy Classification and Use

### Occupancy Groups Overview

Critical for determining MEP system requirements:

| Group | Occupancy Type | MEP Implications |
|-------|---------------|------------------|
| **A** | Assembly | High ventilation rates, emergency lighting, mass notification |
| **B** | Business | Standard office HVAC, IT/telecom infrastructure |
| **E** | Educational | IAQ requirements, security systems, energy recovery |
| **F** | Factory/Industrial | Process exhaust, high electrical loads, hazardous area classification |
| **H** | High Hazard | Specialized HVAC, explosion-proof electrical, fire suppression |
| **I** | Institutional | Life safety systems, backup power, medical gas |
| **M** | Mercantile | Commercial HVAC, retail electrical loads |
| **R** | Residential | Dwelling unit ventilation, residential electrical service |
| **S** | Storage | Warehouse HVAC, material handling electrical |
| **U** | Utility/Miscellaneous | Accessory occupancies |

### Mixed Occupancy Requirements (Section 508)

**Separated Occupancies** (508.3):
- Fire-resistance-rated assemblies
- MEP penetrations must maintain fire ratings
- Separate HVAC systems may be required

**Nonseparated Occupancies** (508.4):
- Most restrictive occupancy provisions apply
- Single HVAC system acceptable with proper design

**Accessory Occupancies** (508.2):
- Subordinate to principal occupancy
- Limited to 10% of building area per floor
- Example: Electrical/mechanical rooms in office building

## Chapter 4: Special Detailed Requirements

### Section 403: High-Rise Buildings

**Definition**: Buildings with occupied floor more than 75 feet above lowest level of fire department vehicle access

**MEP Requirements**:

1. **Emergency Power** (403.4.8):
   - Emergency voice/alarm communication system
   - Exit signs and means of egress illumination
   - Elevator car lighting
   - Emergency responder radio system
   - Fire command center

2. **Standby Power** (403.4.9):
   - Power and lighting for fire command center
   - At least one elevator serving all floors
   - HVAC systems for smokeproof enclosures

3. **Fire Service Access Elevator** (403.6.1):
   - Required for occupied floors >120 feet above grade
   - Dedicated electrical circuit from emergency power
   - Shunt trip protection

4. **Smokeproof Enclosures** (403.5.4):
   - Pressurization or natural ventilation required
   - Dedicated HVAC systems

### Section 404: Atriums

**MEP Coordination Requirements**:

1. **Smoke Control** (404.5):
   - Engineered smoke control system required
   - HVAC integration for smoke exhaust/pressurization
   - Emergency power for smoke control equipment
   - Acceptance testing required per IMC Chapter 5

2. **Fire Detection** (404.4):
   - Automatic smoke detection throughout atrium
   - Activation triggers smoke control system
   - HVAC shutdown or smoke mode activation

### Section 406: Motor Vehicle-Related Occupancies

**Parking Garage Ventilation** (406.6.2):
- Mechanical ventilation: 0.75 cfm per square foot
- Natural ventilation: uniformly distributed openings on two sides
- Electrical classification: ordinary locations (not Class I)

**Electric Vehicle Charging** (406.10):
- Dedicated branch circuits per NFPA 70
- Load calculations per NEC Article 625
- Ventilation for battery charging rooms

## Chapter 7: Fire and Smoke Protection Features

### Section 707: Fire Barriers

**MEP Penetrations** (707.8):
- Penetrations must be protected per Section 714
- Membrane penetrations limited
- Fire dampers required in HVAC ducts

### Section 709: Fire Partitions

**Duct and Air Transfer Openings** (709.6):
- Ducts and air transfer openings comply with Section 717
- Fire/smoke dampers per IMC Section 607

### Section 714: Fire-Resistance-Rated Assemblies

**Through-Penetrations** (714.3):

| Assembly Rating | Penetration Protection |
|----------------|----------------------|
| 1 hour or less | 1-hour F rating |
| Greater than 1 hour | 2-hour F rating |
| Horizontal assemblies | T rating required |

**Membrane Penetrations** (714.4):
- Recessed fixtures in fire-rated ceilings
- Electrical boxes and devices
- Maintain fire resistance

**Firestop Systems** (714.3.1):
- Listed and labeled assemblies
- Installed per manufacturer instructions
- Common MEP penetrations:
  - Electrical conduit and cable trays
  - Plumbing pipes
  - HVAC ducts
  - Data/telecom cables

### Section 717: Ducts and Air Transfer Openings

**Fire Dampers** (717.3):
- Required in fire barriers, fire partitions, smoke barriers
- 1.5-hour or 3-hour rating based on assembly
- Access required for inspection and maintenance

**Smoke Dampers** (717.4):
- Required in smoke barriers per Section 710
- Leakage rating: Class II or III
- Activation by smoke detection system

**Combination Fire/Smoke Dampers** (717.5):
- Dual function for fire and smoke protection
- Common in hospital and healthcare facilities

## Chapter 9: Fire Protection and Life Safety Systems

### Section 901: General

**Applicability**: Systems required by IBC or adopted standards (NFPA 13, 14, 20, 72, etc.)

### Section 903: Automatic Sprinkler Systems

**Sprinkler System Standards**:
- NFPA 13: Standard for Installation of Sprinkler Systems
- NFPA 13R: Residential Occupancies up to Four Stories
- NFPA 13D: One- and Two-Family Dwellings

**Where Required** (903.2):

| Occupancy | Requirement |
|-----------|------------|
| Group A (Assembly) | Varies by occupant load and fire area |
| Group B (Business) | Fire area >12,000 sf or >3 stories |
| Group E (Educational) | Fire area >20,000 sf |
| Group F-1 (Factory) | Fire area >12,000 sf or >3 stories |
| Group H (High Hazard) | All Group H occupancies |
| Group I (Institutional) | All Group I occupancies |
| Group M (Mercantile) | Fire area >12,000 sf or >3 stories |
| Group R (Residential) | Varies by R occupancy subtype |
| Group S-1 (Storage) | Fire area >12,000 sf or >3 stories |

**Design and Installation** (903.3):
- Hydraulically calculated systems required
- NFPA 13 density/area curves
- Water supply analysis required

**MEP Coordination**:
- Plumbing: domestic water separation, backflow prevention
- Electrical: fire pump power supply per NFPA 20
- Structural: seismic bracing per NFPA 13
- Architectural: sprinkler coverage at obstructions

### Section 904: Alternative Automatic Fire-Extinguishing Systems

**System Types**:
- Wet chemical systems (commercial cooking)
- Dry chemical systems
- Gaseous agent systems (data centers, electrical rooms)
- Clean agent systems (FM-200, Novec 1230)
- Water mist systems

**Design Standards**:
- NFPA 17: Dry Chemical Systems
- NFPA 17A: Wet Chemical Systems
- NFPA 2001: Clean Agent Systems

### Section 907: Fire Alarm and Detection Systems

**Required Locations** (907.2):

| Occupancy | Triggering Condition |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Group A | Occupant load >300 or >1000 occupants |
| Group B | Fire area >50,000 sf or >500 occupants above or below discharge |
| Group E | All educational occupancies |
| Group F | Fire area >75,000 sf or >500 occupants above or below discharge |
| Group H | All high-hazard occupancies |
| Group I | All institutional occupancies |
| Group M | Occupant load >500 or >100 occupants above or below discharge |
| Group R | All R-1 hotels, R-2 buildings >2 stories or >16 units |
| Group S | Fire area >75,000 sf or >500 occupants above or below discharge |

**System Components** (907.2.12, 907.5):
- Manual fire alarm boxes (pull stations)
- Automatic detection (smoke, heat, flame)
- Audible notification appliances (75 dBA minimum)
- Visible notification appliances (15-110 candela strobes)
- Voice evacuation systems (high-rise, large assembly)

**Design and Installation** (907.6):
- NFPA 72: National Fire Alarm and Signaling Code
- Device spacing per NFPA 72 Chapter 17
- Circuit supervision and integrity
- Secondary power supply (24-hour capacity)

**MEP Integration**:
- HVAC shutdown or smoke control activation
- Elevator recall
- Door release/magnetic hold-open
- Emergency lighting activation
- Access control integration

### Section 909: Smoke Control Systems

**Where Required**:
- Atriums (Section 404)
- Underground buildings (Section 405)
- Covered mall buildings (Section 402)
- High-rise buildings (optional design alternative)

**Design Requirements** (909.4):
- Rational analysis or accepted engineering methods
- Computer fire modeling acceptable (FDS, CONTAM)
- Pressurization or exhaust methods
- Minimum pressure differences: 0.05 inches water column

**System Components**:
- Smoke exhaust fans (dedicated, emergency power)
- Supply air fans (make-up air)
- Fire/smoke dampers
- Control system and sensors
- Smoke management sequence of operations

**Testing and Commissioning** (909.20):
- Pre-functional testing
- Functional performance testing
- Acceptance testing with authority having jurisdiction
- 10-year periodic testing

**MEP Coordination Critical**:
- Dedicated ductwork and fans
- Emergency power sizing and distribution
- Integration with fire alarm system
- Control sequence programming
- Seismic and wind load design

## Chapter 10: Means of Egress

### Section 1006: Number of Exits and Exit Access Doorways

**MEP Implications**:
- Emergency lighting required along egress paths
- Exit signs at all required exits
- Fire alarm notification appliances in exit corridors

### Section 1009: Stairways and Handrails

**Exit Stairway Lighting** (1009.3):
- Minimum 10 foot-candles at tread
- Emergency lighting per Section 1008

### Section 1013: Exit Access Travel Distance

**Sprinkler Increase** (1017.2):
- Unsprinklered travel distance: typically 200-250 feet
- Sprinklered travel distance: typically 250-300 feet
- Demonstrates sprinkler/egress interrelationship

## Chapter 11: Accessibility (ADA Compliance)

### Section 1103: Scoping Requirements

**Accessible Route** (1104):
- Minimum width: 36 inches
- MEP equipment in accessible route: 80-inch clearance
- Protruding objects limited

### Section 1109: Toilet and Bathing Facilities

**MEP Requirements**:
- Accessible fixture counts and spacing
- Grab bar blocking required
- Sensor-operated fixtures preferred
- Water closet flush controls: 44 inches max height

### Section 1110: Drinking Fountains

**Dual Height Requirement**:
- Standing height: 38-43 inches to spout
- Wheelchair height: 36 inches max to spout
- Electrical for refrigerated units

### Section 1111: Lavatories and Sinks

**Clearance Requirements**:
- Knee clearance: 27 inches min height, 8 inches depth
- Toe clearance: 9 inches min height
- Hot water pipe insulation required
- Sensor faucets: activation within reach range

**Adopted Standard**: NFPA 70 National Electrical Code (NEC)

**IBC Modifications**:
- Emergency and standby power systems (Section 2702)
- Emergency responder radio coverage (Section 510)

### Section 2702: Emergency and Standby Power Systems

**Emergency Systems** (2702.1):
- Exit signs and means of egress illumination
- Fire alarm systems
- Emergency voice/alarm communications
- Elevator car lighting
- Accessible means of egress (LULA elevators, platform lifts)

**Standby Systems** (2702.2):
- Smoke control systems
- Standpipe and sprinkler fire pumps
- Elevator operation
- Ventilation and smoke removal

**Load Transfer Time**:
- Emergency systems: 10 seconds max
- Standby power: varies by application

## Chapter 30: Elevators and Conveying Systems

### Section 3001: General

**Adopted Standards**:
- ASME A17.1: Safety Code for Elevators and Escalators
- ASME A18.1: Safety Standard for Platform Lifts and Stairway Chairlifts

### Section 3002: Hoistway Enclosures

**Fire-Resistance Rating** (3002.2):
- Hoistway enclosures: 1 or 2-hour rating based on stories connected
- MEP penetrations must maintain rating

### Section 3003: Emergency Operations

**Firefighters' Emergency Operation** (3003.1):
- Phase I: Elevator recall to designated level
- Phase II: In-car firefighter control
- Fire alarm system integration required

### Section 3006: Machine Rooms

**MEP Coordination**:
- Dedicated electrical service from building main
- HVAC for equipment cooling (temperature limits per ASME A17.1)
- Lighting: 200 lux (20 fc) minimum
- Waterproofing to prevent sprinkler water damage
- Access for maintenance (min 28 x 84 inches)

### Section 3008: Occupant Evacuation Elevators

**Application**: Optional means of egress in high-rise buildings

**MEP Requirements**:
- Standby power for full evacuation cycle
- Water protection (hoistway, machine room, lobbies)
- Two-way communication system
- Fire alarm system integration
- Shunt trip per NFPA 70

## Design and QA/QC Application

### Code Review Checklist

**Occupancy Classification**:
- [ ] Occupancy groups correctly identified
- [ ] Mixed occupancy separation or combined provisions applied
- [ ] Accessory occupancies properly classified

**Fire and Life Safety**:
- [ ] Sprinkler system scope matches occupancy thresholds
- [ ] Fire alarm system required locations verified
- [ ] Emergency lighting and exit signs per egress requirements
- [ ] Fire-rated penetrations detailed with firestop systems
- [ ] Fire/smoke dampers scheduled in rated assemblies

**High-Rise Requirements** (if applicable):
- [ ] Emergency power system loads identified
- [ ] Standby power for elevators and smoke control
- [ ] Fire service access elevator provided (if >120 feet)
- [ ] Smokeproof enclosure pressurization designed

**Accessibility Compliance**:
- [ ] Accessible fixture counts and clearances
- [ ] Drinking fountain dual heights
- [ ] Accessible route clearances at MEP equipment

**MEP System Integration**:
- [ ] HVAC systems coordinated with fire/smoke damper locations
- [ ] Electrical penetrations maintain fire ratings
- [ ] Plumbing penetrations firestopped correctly
- [ ] Emergency and standby power loads calculated
- [ ] Fire alarm interfaces with HVAC, elevators, doors documented

### Common Plan Review Deficiencies

1. **Missing Fire Dampers**: Ducts penetrate fire-rated assemblies without dampers
2. **Inadequate Firestop Details**: MEP penetrations lack manufacturer-approved systems
3. **Incomplete Emergency Power**: Not all required loads on emergency generator
4. **Smoke Control Inadequate**: Atrium smoke control lacks acceptance testing plan
5. **Accessibility Issues**: Fixture clearances or heights non-compliant
6. **Mixed Occupancy Errors**: Separation requirements not met between occupancies

### Coordination Drawing Requirements

**Fire Protection Plans**:
- Sprinkler coverage overlaid with architectural reflected ceiling
- Fire alarm device locations coordinated with acoustic and lighting analysis
- Smoke control zoning diagram with HVAC ductwork and dampers

**MEP Coordination Models**:
- BIM clash detection at fire-rated assemblies
- 3D visualization of penetrations requiring firestop
- Accessible route verification with overhead MEP
